How to Fix Slow WiFi at Home in 10 Steps Intro Slow WiFi is frustrating, especially when you’re working or streaming. Before you call your ISP, try these 10 fixes. Most take under 5 minutes and cost nothing. 1. Restart Your Router and Modem Unplug both devices for 60 seconds, then plug the modem back in first. Wait for it to connect, then plug in the router. This clears memory leaks and resets the connection. Fixes 30% of slow WiFi issues. 2. Check Your Internet Speed Go to http://speedtest.net on your phone or laptop. Compare it to the speed you pay for. If it’s way lower on every device, the problem is your ISP, not WiFi. 3. Move Your Router to a Central Spot Routers broadcast in all directions. Put it in the center of your home, off the floor, and away from walls and metal objects. Avoid placing it in a cabinet or behind the TV. 4. Reduce Interference Microwaves, cordless phones, and baby monitors use the 2.4 GHz band and kill WiFi speed. Keep your router 2+ meters away from them. Switch devices to 5 GHz if possible. 5. Limit Connected Devices Each device uses bandwidth. Disconnect phones, tablets, and smart TVs you’re not using. Check your router admin page to see what’s connected and kick off unknown devices. 6. Switch to 5 GHz or 6 GHz Band Most modern routers have 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz. 2.4 GHz goes farther but is slower and crowded. 5 GHz and 6 GHz are faster but shorter range. Use 5 GHz when you’re close to the router. 7. Update Router Firmware Log into your router at 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1. Check for firmware updates. Updates fix bugs and improve speed. Most new routers update automatically. 8. Change Your WiFi Channel If you live in an apartment, your neighbors’ routers crowd the same channels. Use an app like WiFi Analyzer to find a less busy channel and change it in router settings. 9. Secure Your Network Use WPA3 or WPA2 encryption with a strong password. An open network lets neighbors use your bandwidth and slows you down. 10. Upgrade Your Equipment If your router is 5+ years old, it’s outdated. WiFi 6 and WiFi 6E routers handle more devices and give faster speeds. You don’t need to replace the ISP modem unless they offer a better one. Conclusion Test your speed after each step. In most cases, steps 1-4 fix the problem. If it’s still slow, contact your ISP and ask them to check the line. - Add 1-2 images of a router and speed test.
